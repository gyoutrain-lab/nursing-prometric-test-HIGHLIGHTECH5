Day 5: Ethical, Legal, & Communication Standards

Question 1

A nurse technician is at lunch in the hospital cafeteria and begins discussing a patient’s colorful behavior with a colleague. This action is a violation of:

  • A. The patient’s right to a private room.
  • B. HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) and patient confidentiality.
  • C. The hospital’s dress code policy.
  • D. The technician’s right to free speech.
Answer: B
Rationale: HIPAA and patient confidentiality rules state that protected health information may only be shared with individuals directly involved in the patient's care and who have a "need to know."
Question 2

A patient refuses to take their scheduled bath, stating they are too tired. What is the most appropriate response by the nurse technician?

  • A. Force the patient to bathe anyway to maintain hospital hygiene standards.
  • B. Respect the patient’s right to refuse and offer to assist them later.
  • C. Tell the patient they cannot have dinner unless they take a bath.
  • D. Call the patient’s family and ask them to scold the patient.
Answer: B
Rationale: Patients have autonomy and the right to refuse care or hygiene. The technician should document the refusal and offer to assist at a later, more convenient time.
Question 3

Which of the following actions by a nurse technician would be considered Invasion of Privacy?

  • A. Closing the curtain before assisting a patient with a bedpan.
  • B. Looking through a patient’s personal purse without their permission.
  • C. Knocking on the door before entering a patient's room.
  • D. Covering a patient with a bath blanket during a bed bath.
Answer: B
Rationale: Patients retain rights to their personal items. Searching a patient's belongings without a clinical reason or consent violates their privacy rights.
Question 4

A nurse technician is asked by a patient's friend over the phone for an update on the patient’s condition. The technician should:

  • A. Give a detailed report of the patient’s vital signs and lab results.
  • B. Tell the friend the patient is doing poorly to encourage a visit.
  • C. Politely inform the caller that information cannot be shared without the patient's consent and transfer the call to the nurse.
  • D. Hang up immediately.
Answer: C
Rationale: To comply with confidentiality guidelines, you must verify authorization before releasing information to anyone, including friends or family members.
Question 5

A nurse technician sees the name of a neighbor on the patient assignment sheet and looks up the neighbor's diagnosis out of curiosity. This is:

  • A. Acceptable since they live on the same street.
  • B. A minor administrative issue.
  • C. A terminable offense and serious breach of confidentiality.
  • D. Permitted if the neighbor verbally asked about it earlier.
Answer: C
Rationale: Accessing any medical record without a direct clinical assignment and a professional need to know is a severe confidentiality violation that can result in termination.
Question 6

A patient wishes to leave the hospital against medical advice (AMA). The nurse technician should first:

  • A. Block the door to prevent the patient from leaving.
  • B. Inform the licensed nurse immediately so the appropriate forms can be signed.
  • C. Tell the patient they will be reported to the police.
  • D. Take the patient’s clothes away.
Answer: B
Rationale: Patients have a legal right to leave. The licensed nurse and physician must be notified so the patient is informed of the risks before signing out AMA.
Question 7

While bathing a patient, the nurse technician and a colleague begin to argue about a clinical assignment in front of the patient. This action violates:

  • A. The hospital's fire safety code.
  • B. Professional boundaries and the patient's right to a calm, dignified environment.
  • C. The patient's right to refuse treatment.
  • D. Standard precautions for infection control.
Answer: B
Rationale: Arguing in front of patients creates an unprofessional environment and increases patient stress. Conflicts must be resolved privately.
Question 8

When a patient is making discriminatory or inappropriate remarks to the nurse technician, the most appropriate professional response is to:

  • A. Make a sarcastic remark back.
  • B. Set professional limits, remain calm, and report the behavior to the nurse in charge.
  • C. Stop providing care entirely without telling anyone.
  • D. Ignore it and verbally degrade the patient to other staff.
Answer: B
Rationale: Technicians are expected to maintain professional boundaries, ensure their own safety, and report abusive or inappropriate behavior to the nurse in charge.
Question 9

A hospital displays a sign outlining patient rights near the main entrance. Which regulatory standard does this fulfill?

  • A. National Fire Protection Association standards.
  • B. Regulatory compliance for patient rights and responsibilities.
  • C. The laboratory safety guidelines.
  • D. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) requirements.
Answer: B
Rationale: Healthcare facilities are legally required to inform patients of their rights and display them prominently.
Question 10

A Muslim patient requests that their bed be oriented toward Mecca during prayer times. What is the most appropriate action by the technician?

  • A. Tell the patient that all hospital beds face the same direction.
  • B. Politely assist in turning the bed if the room layout permits, respecting the patient's spiritual needs.
  • C. Ignore the request because it is not a medical intervention.
  • D. Tell the patient to wait until visiting hours are over.
Answer: B
Rationale: Respecting cultural and religious rituals is an essential component of total patient care and shows respect for the patient's dignity.

Day 6: Specialist Nursing Care—Maternity, Pediatrics, & Mental Health

Question 11

A nurse technician is assisting a mother with postpartum care. The technician notices the mother’s bedsheet is soaked with a large amount of bright red blood shortly after delivery. What is the priority action?

  • A. Change the sheets quickly so the mother stays dry.
  • B. Massage the mother's abdomen vigorously.
  • C. Notify the nurse immediately of the heavy bleeding.
  • D. Tell the mother this is normal after having a baby.
Answer: C
Rationale: Heavy, bright red bleeding shortly after delivery can be a sign of postpartum hemorrhage. The nurse must assess the patient immediately.
Question 12

When weighing a newborn infant in the nursery, which safety measure is most important for the nurse technician to follow?

  • A. Leave the infant on the scale to go document the weight.
  • B. Keep one hand hovering just above the infant at all times to prevent falls.
  • C. Weigh the infant with their heavy winter clothes on.
  • D. Place the scale on the floor to make it safer.
Answer: B
Rationale: Newborns can move unexpectedly. Keeping a hand close (hovering) without touching the scale ensures the safety of the infant.
Question 13

A nurse technician is assigned to the Pediatric unit. A 4-year-old child is crying for their parents. Which action by the technician is most helpful?

  • A. Tell the child that "big kids don't cry."
  • B. Offer a favorite toy or engage the child in a simple distraction like a storybook.
  • C. Lock the door so the child cannot run away.
  • D. Ignore the crying so the child learns to be independent.
Answer: B
Rationale: Pediatric patients experience separation anxiety. Age-appropriate distraction helps soothe the child and reduces emotional distress.
Question 14

In a Mental Health unit, a patient begins to pace rapidly and speak in a loud, aggressive tone. What is the priority action?

  • A. Challenge the patient's statements to show them they are wrong.
  • B. Ensure personal safety, provide space, and notify the nurse immediately.
  • C. Restrain the patient using bed sheets.
  • D. Leave the unit and abandon the patient.
Answer: B
Rationale: Rapid pacing and a loud voice are warning signs of potential aggression. The technician must prioritize safety and seek assistance from licensed staff.
Question 15

An infant in the nursery has an oxygen saturation monitor on its foot. The nurse technician notices a bluish tint around the infant's lips. The technician should:

  • A. Wait 15 minutes to see if the color changes.
  • B. Immediately report the finding to the nurse.
Answer: B
Rationale: Central cyanosis (blueness around the core or lips) is an indicator of poor oxygenation and requires immediate assessment.
Question 16

A 6-month-old infant is crying and has a high temperature. The nurse technician is told to assist with a tepid sponge bath. The primary purpose of this intervention is to:

  • A. Induce shivering to increase the body temperature.
  • B. Help reduce the body temperature safely without causing shivering.
  • C. Clean the skin using harsh chemicals.
  • D. Wrap the baby tightly to keep them warm.
Answer: B
Rationale: Tepid sponge baths reduce fever without the dangerous side effects of rapid cooling or shivering.
Question 17

When caring for a patient experiencing auditory hallucinations, the nurse technician should:

  • A. Agree with the voices to make the patient feel comfortable.
  • B. Argue with the patient and tell them the voices are not real.
  • C. Acknowledge the patient's feelings without validating the hallucination, and keep the environment calm and safe.
  • D. Leave the patient completely isolated in the dark.
Answer: C
Rationale: Validating the delusion is inappropriate, but arguing can increase patient agitation. Acknowledging their feelings is a therapeutic way to maintain trust.
Question 18

A pediatric patient with extensive burns requires a bath and linen change. Because children lose heat faster than adults, what is a critical safety consideration during the procedure?

  • A. Keep the room cold to reduce bacterial growth.
  • B. Expose the entire body at once to complete the task quickly.
  • C. Minimize the exposed body surface area and keep the room warm to prevent hypothermia.
  • D. Use hot water to clean the wounds.
Answer: C
Rationale: Pediatric patients are at high risk for cold stress and hypothermia, particularly when injured or burned.
Question 19

A patient in a psychiatric unit has a small glass vase brought by visitors. The nurse technician removes the item because it is:

  • A. Not permitted by the hospital's food policy.
  • B. A potential hazard for self-harm or injury.
  • C. Too expensive to keep in a hospital room.
  • D. Banned due to infection control standards.
Answer: B
Rationale: Glass can be broken and presents a self-harm or violence risk in a mental health setting.
Question 20

When changing the gown of a patient who has an IV infusing on an electronic infusion pump, the nurse technician should:

  • A. Disconnect the tubing from the machine to pass the sleeve through.
  • B. Thread the IV bag and tubing through the arm of the gown without disconnecting the setup.
  • C. Pull the IV forcefully through the gown.
  • D. Ask the patient to remove the IV themselves.
Answer: B
Rationale: Technicians must maintain a closed system. Disconnecting the IV line introduces infection risks.

Day 7: Comprehensive Final Review & "Test-Trapping" Nuances

Question 21

A nurse technician is assigned to four patients. Which of the following findings should be reported to the nurse immediately?

  • A. A patient with a cast complaining of an itch under the plaster.
  • B. A patient with a pulse rate that has increased from 72 to 110 beats per minute.
  • C. A patient who refused their breakfast tray because they "weren't hungry."
  • D. A patient who requested a fresh glass of water.
Answer: B
Rationale: A pulse jump in pulse to 110 beats per minute indicates tachycardia, which is a physiological change that takes precedence over routine comfort complaints.
Question 22

Which task is outside the scope of practice for a nurse technician and should be politely declined?

  • A. Taking a rectal temperature on an adult patient.
  • B. Applying a simple, non-sterile dry dressing to a minor skin tear.
  • C. Adjusting the flow rate of an intravenous (IV) medication pump.
  • D. Assisting a patient with a history of stroke to use a walker.
Answer: C
Rationale: Adjusting IV medication flow rates requires nursing licensure and advanced pharmacological training.
Question 23

When answering a question about "Priority," the technician should remember that physiological needs come first. Which of these is the highest priority?

  • A. Helping a patient find their lost eyeglasses.
  • B. Ensuring a patient with difficulty swallowing is sitting upright during a meal.
  • C. Emptying a full urinary drainage bag.
  • D. Changing a soiled bedsheet for a patient who is sleeping.
Answer: B
Rationale: Difficulty swallowing (dysphagia) presents an immediate risk of aspiration and airway compromise.
Question 24

A technician is asked to collect a mid-stream (clean-catch) urine specimen. What is the most important instruction to give the patient before collection?

  • A. Collect the very first part of the stream.
  • B. Clean the genital area, begin to urinate, stop briefly, and then collect the mid-stream urine in the cup.
  • C. Ensure the container is filled to the outer rim.
  • D. Do not use the antiseptic wipes provided.
Answer: B
Rationale: Mid-stream collection is designed to wash away transient flora from the urethral opening, minimizing sample contamination.
Question 25

During post-mortem care, the nurse technician must treat the body with dignity. Which action demonstrates this principle?

  • A. Leaving the room with the lights turned off and the door open.
  • B. Cleaning the body, replacing soiled dressings, and positioning the body to look peaceful for the family.
  • C. Rushing through the process to leave the shift early.
  • D. Telling jokes while preparing the body for the morgue.
Answer: B
Rationale: Post-mortem care is the final act of dignity and respect. Preparing the body appropriately supports the family's grieving process.
Question 26

To prevent shearing forces while repositioning an immobile patient in bed, the nurse technician must:

  • A. Slide the patient across the bottom sheet.
  • B. Use a draw sheet or slide sheet to lift rather than drag the patient.
  • C. Acknowledge and follow safe lifting guidelines by asking the patient to stand without assistance.
  • D. Lower the head of the bed completely before moving the patient.
Answer: B
Rationale: Shearing and friction cause skin breakdown. Using a draw sheet protects the delicate layers of the patient's skin.
Question 27

When measuring an adult patient's blood pressure, the nurse technician inflates the cuff too slowly. What effect will this have on the reading?

  • A. It will create a falsely high reading.
  • B. It will result in a falsely low systolic reading.
  • C. It will have no effect on the measurement.
  • D. It will make it impossible to determine the diastolic pressure.
Answer: B
Rationale: Inflating the cuff too slowly causes venous congestion in the arm, leading to false low systolic pressure recordings.
Question 28

When using an electronic pulse oximeter on a patient, the nurse technician notices the patient is wearing dark nail polish. What is the most appropriate action?

  • A. The technician should remove the polish before applying the probe.
  • B. The technician should move the sensor to an alternate site, such as the earlobe.
  • C. The technician should record the reading as normal.
  • D. The technician should hold the patient's finger still.
Answer: B
Rationale: Dark or metallic nail polish interferes with light absorption in the pulse oximeter sensor, requiring an alternative site measurement.
Question 29

A patient with known suicidal ideation is on 1-to-1 observation. The nurse technician leaves the room briefly to get a cup of water. This action represents:

  • A. An acceptable break in observation.
  • B. A serious breach of protocol that endangers the patient.
  • C. Standard procedure for personal breaks.
  • D. A task that can be delegated to housekeeping.
Answer: B
Rationale: Constant observation requires unbroken visual contact. Leaving the patient unattended could result in self-harm or a medical emergency.
Question 30

On a multiple-choice examination, if the test-taker is unsure about the correct answer, the best strategy is to:

  • A. Leave the answer sheet blank.
  • B. Choose the longest or most detailed option.
  • C. Carefully read the question stem for clues, use the elimination method, and ensure that no question is left blank.
  • D. Change the answer to the first one selected.
Answer: C
Rationale: Leaving an answer blank guarantees no points. Eliminating wrong choices and making an educated guess maximizes the chances of a correct response.
